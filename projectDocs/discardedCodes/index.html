CSR

Cancer, as we all know, is a dreadful disease. Unless it is detected very early and appropriately treated, prognosis is not very bright in most cases. Treatment is long, expensive and is usually accompanied by host of distressing symptoms, physical, psychosocial, emotional and spiritual as well. The entire family, as a unit, is drained financially and emotionally. If the patient is poor or young in age, problems get further compounded. The journey from detection of the disease, through its treatment, remission, re-occurrence, re-treatment, end-of-life stage and finally death…all of it is full of physical, psychological, emotional and spiritual struggles.
 

Palliative Care
Palliative care is a multi-disciplinary team approach, envisaging medicine, physiotherapy, psychology, sociology & spiritual care, which aims at reducing physical, psychological, emotional and spiritual pain of patients and their family members. It is a symptomatic care wherein all distressing symptoms, physical and non-physical, are addressed.
 

Hospice Care
A Hospice literally means a homelike facility to provide supportive palliative care to advanced stage cancer patients when their disease is unresponsive to curative treatment. In this stage severe distressing symptoms like pain, nausea, bleeding etc. can manifest. It may not be always possible to address all such symptoms at home. Under such a situation, Hospice Care is recommended. Such care is based on the ethos of care and not cure, for cure may have a limit but not care.
 

Home Care
Often patients do not wish to get admitted for in-patient care at the hospice. However, they continue to suffer at home for want of proper palliative care/treatment. Many of them are too poor to go to a GP for resolution of their symptoms. And even if they do go, a GP being hardly trained for palliative care, they do not get appropriate care/treatment.
Snehaanchal has a Home Care team consisting of a trained Nurse, Nursing Aid and a Counsellor. The team is able to visit 5 – 6 homes per day. Nursing care is provided at home by the Nurse and medicines are advised under guidance from Doctor at hospice. The counsellor provides psychosocial & emotional support to patients & their family.

About Snehaanchal

Snehaanchal Palliative Care Centre is a registered charitable trust working hand-in-hand with cancer sufferers and their families, providing all possible support in their hour of need. Our team of doctors, nurses, psychologists, counselors, and volunteers collectively offer holistic care to the distressed patients and their families, until and even after bereavement. As the name suggests, the team provides a loving, comforting and supportive cover of care. Hence the name ‘Snehaanchal’.
Set up at Nagpur in technical co-operation with Rashtrasant Tukdoji Regional Cancer Hospital in 2006, it has been serving cancer patients and their families since then. All services here, including medicines for symptomatic relief, stay & food, etc., are provided free of charge and irrespective of caste, creed, religion, gender or nationality.

services

In-patient care

In-Patient Palliative Care is provided by our multi-disciplinary team of palliative care doctors, nurses, psychologists, and social workers. This team works in tandem with treating doctors to provide an extra layer of supportive care.

Palliative Care, as a medical specialty, focuses on providing relief from:

  •  Physical distress such as pain, nausea, vomiting, constipation, bleeding, malodor, insomnia, etc.
  •  Psychological distress such as anger, fear, depression, anxiety, etc.
  •  Social distress such as isolation, loneliness & abandonment by family, etc.
  •  Emotional distress caused by hate, guilt, lack of love, desire to express love, etc.
  •  Spiritual distress caused by questions pertaining to God, transcendence, after-life, etc.

Even while on active curative treatment, admission is given to relieve the patient from acute distressing symptoms. A patient can also be admitted for a shorter duration only for the purpose of providing some respite to family members who might be sick themselves or are burnt-out because of constant care giving.

home care

A disease like cancer causes immense physical pain, psychological trauma, and helplessness to the patients and their families. However, not all cancer patients need to be admitted to Snehaanchal. In many situations, when the pain and distressing symptoms arising out of the treatment are not very acute, the patient can be well looked after at home by our Home Care Team. Further,counseling and psychosocial care can begin from the very day the patient/family becomes aware of the disease. Our team of specialized doctors, psychologists, nurses, and counselors visit the homes of such patients regularly to provide:

  •  Nursing Care
  •  Medical Care for pain & other symptoms
  •  Emotional & Psychological Support
  •  Right Guidance to deal with the disease

OPD facility

Out-Patient Care Department- OPD
OPD facility is provided by experienced doctors, nurses, psychologists and counselors to cancer patients and their families. Patient and their families meet with the team and receive medical, psychosocial & emotional support. They also receive information & advice about other support services.

general faq

What is the nature of the service/care provided by a hospice?

The first goal is to relieve the patient of pain and any distressing symptoms. Once this is achieved, we help them and their family members to come to terms with the disease and its prognosis. The aim is to gradually make them accept the final eventuality so that they are at peace with themselves, their families, and their God.

What services are not provided by the hospice?

Treatment methods of cancer, like surgery, chemotherapy, radiation therapy, etc., are not provided at the hospice. Further, complementary medicine, proton therapy, clinical trials, and cutting-edge technologies are also not provided in a hospice.No life-extending measures, like a ventilator, blood transfusion, and oxygen are provided. The philosophy of end-life-care envisages that no life-limiting or life-extending measures will be deployed.

What are opiates and what is morphine? Is there a possibility of getting addicted to morphine?

Opiates, like Morphine, are the best drugs for controlling severe pain. Opiates help the patient to get relief from severe pain when all the other painkillers prove ineffective. Opiates like Morphine do not cause addiction when used to relieve cancer pain. In fact, if the pain is substantially reduced and the patient feels fine, it may not be necessary to continue with morphine as other painkillers may suffice.

What are the other misconceptions about opiates and morphine?

Other misconceptions:

  • That large doses of morphine are administered to patients:The patient is put on morphine only when other painkillers prove ineffective. Doses are set by doctors according to the level of pain experienced by the patient and as per WHO guidelines for pain management. It is administered after close observation and careful assessment of the patient’s pain. Not less, not more.
  • That patients become drowsy:It is not true in every case. Most patients are very alert and active all the time after consuming opiates.
What charges are payable for Snehaanchal Hospice service?

Snehaanchal does not charge any money from the patient. All services provided by it, including medicines, food, stay, care, etc., are completely free. Food (free of charge) is presently provided to patients, whereas for the caregivers, food is currently provided by voluntary groups of people. Stay is free of charge for family members.

What are the criteria of admission to the hospice? When is the best time to get admitted to a hospice?

The criteria are:

  • The patient must be a cancer sufferer facing severe distressing physical, psychosocial & emotional symptoms.
  • Sometimes a patient can be admitted for short durations to provide respite to the family members or caregivers.
Who provides medical nursing care in a hospice? Who provides psychosocial counseling and support?

Doctors who are especially trained and experienced in thepalliative treatment and care provide medical care.Sisters, Catholic nuns of SMMI order, who are also well-qualified/experienced in nursing, provide nursing care.A trained and experienced psychologist or social worker provides psychosocial counseling.

 

What kind of nutrition is provided in a hospice?

Hygienically prepared, nutritious& balanced diet is provided to the patients. Family members do not need to bring food for the patients. Patients can also req for a special food of their liking. However, only vegetarian food is provided.

 

Are the relatives allowed to stay at the hospice & is food provided to them?

Yes, the relatives are allowed to stay with the patients. There is also a dormitory for the relatives of outstation patients. Food is also provided to the relatives by voluntary groups of people.

 

Once admitted, can a person take discharge from the hospice & go home?

Yes, the patient is always free to go back to their home whenever they feel like it. If the family members are willing to take the patient home, the patient is allowed to go back home.

Before sending the patient home, the caregivers at hospice ensure that family members are fully trained to do wound-dressing, feeding, managing catheter, etc., so that they face no difficulty on this count while at home.

Can a discharged patient seek re-admission to the hospice?

Yes, hospice is more like a home for a patient and if the distressing symptoms re-appear, the patient is always welcomed back.

What is done in the event of death of a person admitted to the hospice?

The relatives are quickly informed. Also the deceased is given a bath and laid in the room where prayer is undertaken for his or her soul and then handed over to the family.

 

When is the right time to start Home Care?

Palliative care can run complementary to curative treatment.  If the patient, while on curative treatment or otherwise, is suffering from symptoms like pain, vomiting, nausea, bleeding or has a fungating tumor needing regular dressing, then it is advisable to ask for a Home Palliative Care. It must also be sought when the patient is suffering from severe anxiety and depression arising because of the disease, affecting both him/her and his/her family.

Does Snehaanchal Home team provide treatment/cure to the patient with cancer?

Treatment for cancer is not provided at all. Treatment is provided to reduce the distressing symptoms, be it physical or psychological.
